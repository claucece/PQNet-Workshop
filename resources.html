Interested in some open problems? Find them here!.

  1. What is more important when putting Post-Quantum cryptography to network protocols: the increased size or the extra ammount of round-trips?
  2. Will isogeny-based algorithms be usable in the different protocols?
  3. How will certificates (specifically, certificate chains) for TLS change to adapt themselves to post-quantum cryptography?
  4. Is it easier to incorporate post-quantum cryptography for confidentiality or to authentication?
  5. Are there other ways to achieve authentication that don't rely on post-quantum signatures that can be incorporated intro protocols?
  6. How 'big' is a big certificate chain for TLS?
